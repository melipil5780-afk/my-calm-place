Your Distress Tolerance Toolkit

Understanding how to navigate emotional storms

What Are Distress Tolerance Skills?

Distress tolerance skills help you survive intense emotional moments without resorting to harmful behaviors. They're like having a life raft when you're in emotional waters that feel too deep or turbulent.

1

Not Problem-Solving

These skills aren't about fixing the problem‚Äîthey're about getting through the moment when you can't fix it right now.

2

Creating Space

They create distance between intense feelings and your actions, preventing impulsive decisions you might regret.

3

Reducing Suffering

They help you bear pain without adding extra suffering through resistance or judgment.

Skill 5: Radical Acceptance

Stop fighting reality to reduce suffering

üìå The Core Idea (One Sentence)

Radical acceptance means completely acknowledging reality as it is right now, without fighting it, even though you don't like it and didn't choose it.

The Quicksand Metaphor

Fighting Reality (Resistance)

Like struggling in quicksand‚Äîit makes you sink faster.

  • "This shouldn't be happening!"
  • "It's not fair!"
  • "Why me?"
  • Creates extra suffering

Accepting Reality

Like stopping the struggle‚Äîyou can think clearly about escape.

  • "This is happening, even though I hate it."
  • "This is real."
  • "Okay, this is where I am."
  • Reduces suffering, frees energy for effective action

Skill 6: TIPP - The Body Chemistry Reset

Changing your physiology to change your emotions

MEDICAL SAFETY CHECK ‚Äì READ BEFORE USING TIPP

DO NOT use TIPP if you have any of these conditions:

  • Heart conditions or irregular heartbeat
  • Recent heart attack or heart surgery
  • High or low blood pressure
  • Especially if on beta-blockers
  • Raynaud's disease or cold urticaria
  • Respiratory issues (asthma, COPD)
  • Pregnancy (consult doctor first)
  • Eating disorders (can be triggering)

When in doubt: Skip TIPP and use STOP + ACCEPTS instead, or use the Quick Support button.

Safer Alternatives to TIPP:

  • Instead of cold water: Splash cool (not cold) water, use damp cloth on neck
  • Instead of intense exercise: Gentle stretching, walking slowly
  • Instead of breath-holding: Gentle 4-7-8 breathing without holding

For Moments of Extreme Crisis

TIPP is designed for when emotions feel completely overwhelming‚Äîlike a "10/10" on the distress scale. It works by rapidly changing your body's physical state, which in turn can short-circuit intense emotional pain.

Worksheet 6

The TIPP Breakdown

T

Temperature

Change your body temperature to activate the "dive reflex" and calm your nervous system.

Safe ideas: Splash cold water on your face, hold an ice pack to your cheeks or wrists, take a cool shower.

Mild Intense

Selected: Moderate (Cold water splash)

I

Intense Exercise

Engage in short, vigorous activity to burn off adrenaline and release endorphins.

Safe ideas: Run in place for 2 minutes, do jumping jacks, sprint up and down stairs, vigorous dancing.

P

Paced Breathing

Slow, deliberate breathing to regulate your heart rate and calm your mind.

Try 4-7-8 breathing: Inhale for 4 counts, hold for 7, exhale slowly for 8.

4
P

Paired Muscle Relaxation

Tense muscle groups tightly, then release, pairing the release with an exhale.

Sequence: Clench fists (hold 5 sec) ‚Üí exhale and release. Tense shoulders (hold) ‚Üí exhale and drop.

Skill 7: STOP - The Mindful Emergency Brake

Creating space between trigger and reaction

Prevent Impulsive Reactions

The STOP skill is your instant pause button. It's designed to be used in the heat of the moment when you feel an overwhelming urge to act (to argue, to send that text, to escape, to self-harm). It creates vital seconds of space to choose a more thoughtful response.
